5. SITE ACCESS AND OWNER RESPONSIBILITIES

5.1 Owner shall provide Contractor with unobstructed access to the project site during normal working hours (Monday through Friday, 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM, unless otherwise agreed).

Contractor shall correct material design errors at no additional charge when notified in writing within the warranty period.

7.4 Maintenance. Contractor warrants maintenance services will be performed in a professional, workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards.

14. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

14.1 Negotiation. Any dispute, claim, or controversy arising out of or relating to this Contract shall first be submitted to senior representatives of both Parties for good-faith written negotiation for a period of thirty (30) days following written notice describing the dispute in reasonable detail.

14.2 Mediation. If the Parties are unable to resolve the dispute through negotiation within thirty (30) days, either Party may initiate non-binding mediation before a mutually agreed neutral mediator in Salt Lake City, Utah. The Parties shall share mediator fees equally and bear their own attorneys' fees. Mediation shall be completed within sixty (60) days of the mediator's appointment. Participation in mediation in good faith is a condition precedent to arbitration, except where a Party seeks emergency injunctive or equitable relief from a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm.

14.3 Arbitration. If the dispute is not resolved through mediation, either Party may submit it to final, binding arbitration in Salt Lake City, Utah, administered by the American Arbitration Association under its Construction Industry Arbitration Rules then in effect. The arbitrator shall have authority to award any remedy available at law or in equity. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding and may be entered as a judgment in any court of competent jurisdiction.

14.4 The prevailing party in any arbitration or other legal proceeding to enforce this Contract shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs from the non-prevailing party.

14.5 Pending resolution of any dispute, each Party shall continue to perform its obligations under this Contract (except for those obligations directly at issue in the dispute) unless this Contract has been terminated.

15. FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 Neither Party shall be in default or liable to the other for any delay or failure to perform its obligations under this Contract to the extent that such delay or failure is caused by a Force Majeure Event. A "Force Majeure Event" means any circumstance or event beyond a Party's reasonable control, including but not limited to: acts of God; fire, flood, earthquake, storm, hurricane, tornado, or other natural disasters; epidemic, pandemic, or public health emergency; war, invasion, terrorism, riot, insurrection, or civil unrest; government action, law, order, regulation, or embargo; strikes or labor disputes not involving that Party's own direct employees; prolonged or widespread failure of utilities, telecommunications, or supply chains; or extraordinary weather conditions that make performance at the project site unsafe or impracticable.

15.2 The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall: (a) provide written notice to the other Party as promptly as practicable after becoming aware of the event; (b) describe the nature, expected duration, and impact of the event; and (c) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 During a Force Majeure Event, performance obligations and deadlines shall be suspended for the duration of the event and extended by a reasonable period. Owner shall not be required to pay for work not performed during a Force Majeure Event, and Contractor shall not be in breach for delayed performance. Schedule impacts due to Force Majeure events shall be addressed through a written Change Order adjusting project timelines, with no adjustment to the Contract price unless the event also results in documented additional costs.

15.4 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive calendar days, either Party may terminate the affected phase of this Contract upon thirty (30) days' written notice without liability for the delay or non-performance caused by the Force Majeure Event, except that Owner shall pay Contractor for all work satisfactorily completed through the termination date and for non-cancellable material orders.
