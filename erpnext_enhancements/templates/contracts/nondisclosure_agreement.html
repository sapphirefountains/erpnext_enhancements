RECITALS

WHEREAS, in connection with a {% if doc.nda_purpose %}{{ fill(doc.nda_purpose, 34) }}{% else %}proposed business relationship{% endif %} between the Parties (the “Proposed Business Relationship”), the Parties anticipate disclosure of, but are in no way required to disclose, Proprietary Information between the Parties (hereinafter individually “Receiving Party” or “Disclosing Party”). Both parties desire that such information remain confidential in accordance with the provisions contained herein.

NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and obligations contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

“Disclose” or “Disclosure” means: (i) to provide Proprietary Information directly or indirectly, knowingly or unknowingly, to the other party, or (ii) to allow the other party access to, or means to access, Proprietary Information.

“Proprietary Information” is defined for the purposes of this Agreement to include, without limitation, any of the following that are owned by, controlled by, possessed by, or in the custody of, a Party: (i) computer programs; technical drawings; engineering plans; algorithms; know-how; formulas; tools; methodologies; processes; ideas; inventions (whether patentable or not); schematics; technical, business, financial, customer, and product information; development plans; forecasts; strategies; and other information (collectively “Items”); (ii) all communications of any kind concerning the foregoing Items; (iii) information about the foregoing Items communicated and not reduced to a written or printed document and not stored on any electronic or other media; and (iv) information about the foregoing Items not communicated, but otherwise discovered by a Party through access that it would not otherwise have had except for being a Party to this Agreement.

Proprietary Information does not include information: (i) available to the general public at the time it is received by a Party and such public knowledge is not the result of a breach of this Agreement, was obtain unlawfully, or was not a violation of any other legal right; (ii) acquired by a Party from a third party that is not under any confidentiality obligation regarding such information; or (iii) independently developed by a Party without use of any Proprietary Information of the other Party or by employees of that Party who have had no access to Proprietary Information of the other Party.

The Receiving Party hereby agrees that after obtaining any Proprietary Information, in whatever form, the Receiving Party shall: (a) Hold the Disclosing Party’s Proprietary Information in confidence and take all commercially reasonable actions to protect such Proprietary Information (including, without limitation, all precautions the Receiving Party employs with respect to its own confidential and/or proprietary materials); (b) Only Disclose Proprietary Information to employees who need to know such information for purposes of evaluating the Proposed Business Relationship, to retained counsel, and to contractors, accountants, or other third parties who must obtain the Proprietary Information to advise Disclosing Party and who have signed a confidentiality agreement that obligates such persons to comply with the terms of this Agreement; (c) Not make any use whatsoever at any time of such Proprietary Information except to evaluate internally the Proposed Business Relationship; (d) Not copy or reverse engineer any such Proprietary Information; and (e) Not export or re-export (as defined by United States federal laws and/or regulations) any such Proprietary Information or product thereof.

If the Receiving Party is requested or required by oral questions, interrogatories, requests for information or documents, subpoena, civil investigative demand, or other legal or non-legal process, the Receiving Party must notify the Disclosing Party within Forty-Eight (48) hours of receipt of such request or of the Receiving Party’s knowledge of such request, and to the extent practicable, prior to making any Disclosure, to provide the Disclosing Party an opportunity to object to any Disclosure, to obtain a protective order, or to otherwise protect the confidentiality of the Proprietary Information. The Receiving Party must thereafter Disclose no more of the Proprietary Information than is absolutely necessary to comply with the legally required request.

Within Three (3) days of a request by the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party will deliver to the Disclosing Party all Proprietary Information of the Disclosing Party in tangible or stored form (including without limitation all documents and information stored on any media of any kind containing any such Proprietary Information and any and all copies or extracts thereof). If returning Proprietary Information included in this Paragraph 6 is impracticable or is prohibited by law, regulation, or government order, the Receiving Party must keep such Proprietary Information confidential for the Ten (10) years from the date of receiving the Proprietary Information. However, if the Disclosure of Proprietary Information after this ten-year period would reveal a trade secret or would reasonably be anticipated to cause economic or other harm to the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party must keep such Proprietary Information confidential until it is no longer a trade secret and until such harm would not occur.

Each Party acknowledges and agrees: (i) Subject to the last sentence of Section 6, the Parties obligations under this Agreement are binding until ten (10) years after the Effective Date; (ii) any Disclosure of the other Party’s Proprietary Information constitutes a breach of this Agreement; (iii) due to the unique nature of the Disclosing Party’s Proprietary Information, there can be no adequate remedy at law for any breach; (iv) a breach will result in irreparable harm to the other Party; (v) upon any breach or any threat thereof, the non-breaching Party shall be entitled to an injunction, specific performance, or other appropriate equitable relief (including without the requirement of posting a bond, in addition to whatever remedies such Party might have at law. The prevailing Party in any action to enforce this Agreement shall be entitled to its costs (including, but not limited to, reasonable attorneys’ fees).

This Agreement is hereby deemed an integrated agreement. No modification of this Agreement will be binding unless made in writing and signed by each Party. This Agreement shall be binding upon the Parties and their respective parents, subsidiaries, affiliates, directors, officers, employees, agents, advisors, consultants, and representatives of any kind. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Utah without regard to choice of law provisions. The Parties hereby submit to the jurisdiction of the state and federal courts in Utah.
