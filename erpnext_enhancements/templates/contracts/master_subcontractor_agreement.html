1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 This Agreement establishes the general terms and conditions governing all work Subcontractor performs for Company. The specific scope, deliverables, timelines, and compensation for each engagement shall be set forth in a written Statement of Work ("SOW") signed by both Parties and incorporated herein by reference.

1.2 In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and any SOW, the SOW controls for that engagement unless otherwise expressly stated.

1.3 Company provides design, installation, maintenance, and service of water features, fountains, and related aquatic systems. Subcontractor may be engaged to provide professional services (Tier 1) or trade construction services (Tier 2) as indicated above.

2. STATEMENTS OF WORK

2.1 Each engagement shall be governed by a signed SOW specifying: a description of the services; the project site; start and completion dates; compensation; and any engagement-specific requirements.

2.2 No SOW is binding unless signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

2.3 Changes to any SOW require a written, signed amendment before implementation.

3. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

3.1 Subcontractor is an independent contractor — not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of Company — and has no authority to bind Company.

3.2 Subcontractor is solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes on compensation received under this Agreement.

3.3 Subcontractor shall supply all tools, equipment, and materials necessary to perform the work unless otherwise specified in a SOW.

4. COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT

4.1 Company shall pay Subcontractor the amounts set forth in each SOW. Invoices must reference the applicable SOW number and project name.

4.2 Pay If Paid. Subcontractor's right to receive payment from Company is expressly contingent upon Company's receipt of payment from the project owner or general contractor ("Owner") for the work covered by Subcontractor's invoice. If Company has not received payment from Owner through no fault of Company, Company has no obligation to pay Subcontractor for that work until such payment is received. Subcontractor expressly assumes the risk of Owner non-payment as a negotiated allocation of risk. This provision does not apply to delays caused by Company's own breach.

4.3 Payment is due within ten (10) business days of Company's receipt of the corresponding payment from Owner. Subcontractor must dispute any invoice in writing within ten (10) days of receipt.

4.4 For Tier 2 engagements, Company may retain up to ten percent (10%) of each progress payment as retainage, to be released within thirty (30) days of final acceptance of Subcontractor's work.

4.5 Company may withhold payment for work not performed per the SOW, uncorrected defects, unresolved safety violations, absent required submittals, or missing lien waivers. Overdue balances accrue interest at 1.5% per month.

5. INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS

5.1 Condition Precedent — COI Required Before Work Begins. NO WORK SHALL COMMENCE under any SOW until Subcontractor delivers a current Certificate of Insurance (COI) to Company evidencing all required coverages. Company may direct Subcontractor to stop work and withhold payment if a valid COI is not on file at all times. See Exhibit A for complete insurance specifications.

5.2 Required Coverages. Subcontractor shall maintain at its sole expense:

Commercial General Liability: $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate / $2,000,000 products-completed operations — including contractual liability;

Workers' Compensation: statutory limits per Utah law; Employer's Liability: $500,000 per accident / disease / policy limit;

Commercial Auto Liability: $1,000,000 CSL — all owned, hired, and non-owned vehicles;

Professional Liability / E&O (Tier 1 design/engineering work): $1,000,000 per claim / $2,000,000 aggregate; tail coverage minimum 3 years;

Umbrella / Excess (Tier 2): $2,000,000 per occurrence / aggregate, following form over CGL, auto, and employer's liability.

5.3 Sapphire Fountains, LLC shall be named as additional insured on all CGL and umbrella policies on a primary, non-contributory basis (ISO CG 20 10 + CG 20 37 endorsements or equivalent). All policies shall include a waiver of subrogation in Company's favor. Insurers must be A.M. Best A-VII or better and licensed in Utah. Subcontractor shall provide thirty (30) days' prior written notice of cancellation (ten (10) days for non-payment).

6. PERFORMANCE AND PAYMENT BONDS

6.1 If required by the applicable SOW or by written request, Subcontractor shall furnish a Performance Bond and Payment Bond, each at 100% of the SOW value, issued by a Utah-licensed surety with an A.M. Best rating of A-VIII or better, in a form acceptable to Company.

6.2 Required bonds shall be delivered at Subcontractor's expense before work commences. Failure to provide bonds within ten (10) business days of request is grounds for immediate termination under Section 17.

7. LICENSES AND COMPLIANCE

7.1 Subcontractor warrants that it holds all licenses, certifications, and permits required by law to perform the work, and shall maintain them in good standing throughout this Agreement.

7.2 Subcontractor shall comply with all applicable laws, codes, regulations, and standards — including OSHA, applicable building and trade codes, and any site-specific safety requirements.

8. WORKMANSHIP AND WARRANTIES

8.1 Subcontractor warrants that all work shall be performed in a professional, workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards, and that all materials furnished shall be new and free from defects unless otherwise agreed.

8.2 Subcontractor warrants all labor and workmanship for one (1) year from final acceptance (or longer if required by the SOW or applicable law), and shall promptly correct defective work at no cost to Company within forty-eight (48) hours of written notice.

9. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

9.1 All work product, drawings, reports, and deliverables created by Subcontractor under this Agreement are works made for hire and are the sole property of Company upon payment.

9.2 To the extent any work product does not qualify as a work made for hire, Subcontractor irrevocably assigns all right, title, and interest therein to Company.

10. CONFIDENTIALITY

10.1 Subcontractor shall hold all non-public Company information in strict confidence, use it solely to perform services hereunder, and not disclose it to any third party without Company's prior written consent. These obligations survive termination for three (3) years.

11. INDEMNIFICATION

11.1 Subcontractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company and its members, managers, officers, employees, and agents from all claims, losses, damages, costs, and expenses (including attorneys' fees) arising from: (a) Subcontractor's performance; (b) any breach of this Agreement; (c) negligent or intentional acts of Subcontractor or its personnel; (d) violation of any law by Subcontractor; or (e) any lien or stop-notice filed by Subcontractor's lower-tier parties.

11.2 Company shall indemnify Subcontractor against claims arising solely from Company's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

12. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY AND NO DAMAGES FOR DELAY

12.1 Company's total liability to Subcontractor under any SOW shall not exceed the total compensation paid under that SOW in the three (3) months preceding the claim. Neither Party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages.

12.2 No Damages for Delay. Subcontractor's sole remedy for any delay, disruption, or interference — regardless of cause — shall be an equitable time extension documented in a written SOW amendment. No monetary delay damages, extended overhead, or lost productivity shall be payable. This provision does not apply to delays caused by Company's intentional misconduct or bad faith.

13. FLOW-DOWN CLAUSE

13.1 Subcontractor agrees to be bound by the same obligations to Company that Company owes to the project owner under the prime contract ("Prime Contract"), to the extent applicable and not inconsistent with this Agreement. Subcontractor shall have no greater rights against Company than Company has against the project owner.

13.2 All claims requiring owner action must be submitted to Company in sufficient time for Company to include them in its own claim under the Prime Contract.

14. RIGHT TO TAKE OVER

14.1 If Subcontractor fails to diligently prosecute work, maintain the project schedule, correct defective work within 48 hours of notice, comply with safety or insurance requirements, or is otherwise in material default, Company may — after 48 hours' written notice (or immediately in emergencies or upon abandonment) — take over all or any part of the work and complete it using its own forces or other contractors.

14.2 All costs to complete shall be charged against amounts due Subcontractor. Subcontractor remains liable for all costs exceeding the remaining SOW balance. Exercise of this right does not waive any other Company remedy.

15. JOINT CHECK CLAUSE

15.1 Company may issue joint checks payable to Subcontractor and any of its material suppliers or lower-tier subcontractors to protect the project from mechanics' lien exposure. Subcontractor shall execute any joint check agreement within five (5) business days of request and shall not interfere with issuance of joint checks.

16. LABOR DISPUTES

16.1 Subcontractor shall notify Company within twenty-four (24) hours of any actual or threatened labor dispute, strike, lockout, or work stoppage affecting its workforce or any project site, and shall use its best efforts to prevent and resolve such disputes promptly.

16.2 Subcontractor shall indemnify Company from all delays, costs, fines, or claims arising from labor disputes related to Subcontractor's work or Subcontractor's failure to comply with applicable labor laws.

17. TERMINATION

17.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon thirty (30) days' written notice.

17.2 Company may terminate immediately upon written notice if Subcontractor: (a) materially breaches and fails to cure within ten (10) business days; (b) becomes insolvent; (c) loses a required license; (d) fails to provide bonds or insurance; or (e) commits fraud, gross negligence, or willful misconduct.

17.3 Upon termination, Subcontractor is entitled to payment for satisfactorily completed and accepted work through the termination date, less retainage and any costs Company incurs to complete or correct Subcontractor's work.

18. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

18.1 Negotiation. The Parties shall attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith written negotiation for thirty (30) days following written notice of the dispute.

18.2 Mediation. If negotiation fails, the dispute shall be submitted to non-binding mediation before a mutually agreed mediator in Salt Lake City, Utah, with costs shared equally. Mediation is a condition precedent to arbitration, except for emergency equitable relief.

18.3 Arbitration. Disputes unresolved through mediation shall be submitted to final, binding arbitration under the AAA Construction Industry Arbitration Rules in Salt Lake City, Utah. The decision is final and may be entered as a court judgment. The prevailing party recovers reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

19. GOVERNING LAW

19.1 This Agreement is governed by Utah law. Any proceedings not subject to arbitration shall be brought in the state or federal courts of Davis County, Utah.

20. GENERAL PROVISIONS

20.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement together with all SOWs constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior negotiations.

20.2 Amendments. Modifications must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

20.3 Severability. Invalid provisions shall be severed without affecting the remainder.

20.4 Notices. All notices shall be in writing by email (with confirmation), overnight courier, or certified mail.

20.5 Assignment. Subcontractor may not assign this Agreement without Company's prior written consent. Company may assign to any successor entity.

20.6 Force Majeure. Neither Party shall be in default for delays caused by circumstances beyond its reasonable control (acts of God, government orders, natural disasters, pandemic, utility failures), provided prompt written notice is given and commercially reasonable efforts are made to resume performance.

20.7 Electronic Signatures. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including electronically, each deemed an original.

20.8 Personal Guarantee. Company may require an owner or principal of Subcontractor to execute a Personal Guarantee in the form of Exhibit B before work commences under any SOW.

TRADE SUBCONTRACTOR ADDENDUM
Sections 21–24 apply only when Tier 2 is checked on page 1.

21. SHOP DRAWINGS, SAMPLES, AND AS-BUILT DOCUMENTS

21.1 Submittals. Before fabricating or installing any component requiring shop drawings, product data, or samples (as identified in the SOW or by Company), Subcontractor shall submit complete, accurate submittals to Company for review. Company's review is for general design conformance only and does not relieve Subcontractor of responsibility for field dimensions, fabrication, connections, or compliance with contract requirements. Subcontractor shall not proceed with fabrication or installation of items requiring approved submittals until written approval is received; work done prior to approval is at Subcontractor's risk.

21.2 Resubmittals. Rejected or revised items shall be resubmitted within five (5) business days of receiving Company's comments, with all changes clearly identified.

21.3 Samples. Where samples are required, Subcontractor shall submit the specified number for approval before ordering or installing materials. Approved samples shall be maintained on-site as the standard for comparison.

21.4 As-Built Drawings. As a condition of final payment, Subcontractor shall deliver accurate as-built drawings (marked-up redlines or digital files) showing all field deviations from the original construction documents, including actual routing of plumbing, conduit, and piping; actual equipment locations and dimensions; and all field changes approved by Change Order. As-builts shall be signed and dated by Subcontractor's field supervisor in the format specified by Company (PDF, AutoCAD, or equivalent).

21.5 O&M Manuals. Subcontractor shall provide complete operations and maintenance manuals, warranties, and start-up instructions for all installed equipment and systems, as a condition of final payment.

22. SITE RULES AND CONDUCT

22.1 Subcontractor and all its personnel shall comply with all site rules, project requirements, and directives issued by Company or the project owner, and shall ensure each worker is briefed before beginning work.

22.2 Check-In. Subcontractor shall report to Company's designated site superintendent upon arrival each day. No work shall begin without a briefing from Company's site representative.

22.3 Housekeeping. Subcontractor shall maintain a clean, organized, and safe work area at all times, removing all debris and surplus materials from the site at the end of each workday.

22.4 Materials and Equipment. Subcontractor shall store materials only in designated staging areas and shall not block access routes, exits, or fire lanes.

22.5 PPE. All personnel shall wear required PPE (hard hats, high-visibility vests, safety glasses, steel-toed boots) at all times, except as the site risk assessment permits.

22.6 Prohibited Conduct. Strictly prohibited on all project sites: alcohol or controlled substances; firearms or weapons; harassment or discriminatory conduct; unauthorized photography; parking in restricted areas.

22.7 Removal of Personnel. Company may require the immediate removal of any individual from the site for safety violations, misconduct, or repeated rule violations. Subcontractor shall comply without delay.

23. STORMWATER POLLUTION PREVENTION (SWPPP)

23.1 Subcontractor shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local stormwater regulations — including the Clean Water Act, EPA Construction General Permit, and Utah DEQ requirements — to the extent applicable to Subcontractor's scope.

23.2 Subcontractor shall implement all required Best Management Practices (BMPs) to prevent discharge of sediment, concrete washout, fuels, chemicals, and other pollutants from the project site, and shall participate in the project SWPPP or maintain its own as directed by Company.

23.3 In the event of a BMP failure or unauthorized discharge attributable to Subcontractor's operations, Subcontractor shall immediately implement corrective measures, notify Company, and document the actions taken.

23.4 Subcontractor shall indemnify Company from all fines, penalties, cleanup costs, and liabilities arising from Subcontractor's SWPPP non-compliance.

24. CERTIFIED PAYROLL AND PREVAILING WAGES

24.1 If the project is subject to prevailing wage requirements under federal law (Davis-Bacon Act), Utah law, or local ordinance, Subcontractor shall pay all workers at not less than the applicable prevailing rates and fringe benefits, and shall require compliance from all lower-tier subcontractors.

24.2 If required, Subcontractor shall submit certified payroll reports (Form WH-347 or equivalent state form) to Company weekly, within three (3) business days after each payroll period closes. Reports shall be signed and certified by an authorized officer of Subcontractor.

24.3 Subcontractor shall post required prevailing wage notices at the project site, maintain all payroll records for a minimum of three (3) years after final completion, and make records available for inspection upon request.

24.4 Subcontractor shall indemnify Company from all penalties, back wages, fines, and liabilities arising from failure to comply with prevailing wage requirements.
