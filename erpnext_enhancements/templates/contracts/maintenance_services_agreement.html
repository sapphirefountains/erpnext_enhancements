1. PARTIES

This Maintenance Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between Sapphire Fountains, LLC, a Utah limited liability company ("Service Provider"), and the Client identified above ("Client"). Service Provider agrees to provide the maintenance services selected above, and Client agrees to pay for such services in accordance with this Agreement.

2. SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 Standard Maintenance Services. Each scheduled maintenance visit shall include, as applicable to the Client's water feature type:

Inspection of pump(s), motor(s), and mechanical components for proper operation;

Cleaning of pump strainer baskets, skimmers, and filters;

Removal of debris (leaves, algae, sediment) from basin, bowl, and surrounding area;

Testing and adjustment of water chemistry (pH, alkalinity, sanitizer levels);

Addition of water treatment chemicals as needed (cost of chemicals included unless otherwise noted);

Inspection and cleaning of nozzles, jets, and distribution heads;

Inspection of lighting components (bulbs, wiring, fixtures);

Water level check and top-off as needed;

Visual inspection of plumbing connections for leaks;

Documentation of any noted issues or recommended repairs;

Written service report provided to Client following each visit.

2.2 Seasonal Startup Services (Spring). If selected, Service Provider shall perform the following upon spring activation:

Full inspection of all mechanical and electrical components after winter dormancy;

Reinstallation and reconnection of pumps, plumbing, and equipment removed for winter storage;

System flush and initial fill;

Chemical startup treatment and water balancing;

Full operational test of pump, lighting, and all features;

Identification and documentation of any winter damage or components requiring repair or replacement.

2.3 Winterization Services (Fall). If selected, Service Provider shall perform the following to prepare the water feature for winter:

Complete draining of basin, plumbing, and equipment;

Removal, cleaning, and secure storage of pumps, lights, and sensitive components (stored on-site or at Service Provider's facility as agreed);

Blowing out and clearing of all water lines to prevent freeze damage;

Installation of covers or protective materials as applicable;

Documentation of equipment removed and its condition.

2.4 Services NOT Included. The following are not included in standard maintenance and will be quoted separately as needed:

Structural repairs, major equipment replacement, or resurfacing;

Electrical repairs beyond minor bulb replacement;

Plumbing repairs requiring excavation or re-piping;

Removal of invasive vegetation or tree root intrusion;

Water features that are inaccessible without specialized equipment.

15. FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 Neither Party shall be in default or liable to the other for any delay or failure to perform its obligations under this Agreement to the extent that such delay or failure is caused by a Force Majeure Event. A "Force Majeure Event" means any circumstance beyond a Party's reasonable control, including but not limited to: acts of God; fire, flood, earthquake, storm, hurricane, tornado, or other natural disasters; epidemic or pandemic; war, invasion, terrorism, riot, or civil unrest; government action, law, order, or regulation; strikes or labor disputes not involving that Party's own employees; prolonged or widespread failure of utilities or telecommunications infrastructure; or extreme weather conditions rendering site access unsafe.

15.2 The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall: (a) provide written notice to the other Party as soon as reasonably practicable after becoming aware of the event; (b) describe the nature and expected duration of the event; and (c) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects and resume performance as soon as practicable.

15.3 During a Force Majeure Event, Service Provider's obligation to perform and Client's obligation to pay for unperformed services shall both be suspended for the duration of the event. Service Provider shall reschedule suspended visits as promptly as conditions allow. Fees for services not performed due to a Force Majeure Event shall not be charged or shall be credited to Client's account.

15.4 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days, either Party may terminate the affected portion of this Agreement upon written notice without liability, except that Client shall remain responsible for all fees for services actually performed prior to the termination date.
