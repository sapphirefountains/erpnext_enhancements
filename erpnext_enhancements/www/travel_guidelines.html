Sapphire Fountains — General Travel Guidelines

Standard policies and procedures for all employees traveling for company business. The goal of these guidelines is to ensure that all travel is safe, cost-effective, and efficient. Adherence is required unless an exception is pre-approved by management.

1. Booking Accommodations

Your comfort and proximity to the work site are priorities. When booking lodging:

  • Occupancy: all hotel or lodging reservations are to be booked for one adult traveler per room.
  • Location: whenever possible, book within a close and convenient distance to the primary project site — this minimizes daily travel time and associated costs.
In the system Record each reservation as an Accommodation row on the Travel Trip — one row per room, with the row's Traveler set to its occupant, the confirmation number, and the booking attached. Hotel check-ins appear on your mobile itinerary and in your calendar invite automatically.

2. Travel Site Information

For each project, travelers are required to compile the key site information before departing:

  • Identify key locations: research the area around the project site.
  • Locate Home Depot: find the address and contact information for the Home Depot nearest to the project site — crucial for acquiring materials and supplies efficiently.
  • Record information: capture the Home Depot location and any other relevant site details (parking information, site contact, gate codes) so the whole crew can find them.
In the system Site information lives as Travel POI records: add the nearest Home Depot with category Hardware Store (plus Supply Depot / Client HQ / Job Site entries as relevant), put parking and access details in the POI Notes, and set the map pin. Link each POI to an itinerary stop on the Travel Trip — pinned POIs then show on the trip's map and on everyone's mobile itinerary (/itinerary) with Open-in-Maps directions. POIs are reusable: check whether one already exists before creating it.

3. Air Travel

To maximize productivity and minimize travel-related fatigue:

  • Direct flights are strongly preferred and should be booked whenever they are available and the cost is reasonable.
  • Flight times: aim for a departure between 8:00 AM and 5:00 PM in the departure city's time zone. Red-eye flights should be avoided whenever possible so travelers are well-rested.
  • Layovers: if a direct flight is not available or is prohibitively expensive, one layover may be booked. More than one layover requires management approval.
In the system Enter every booked flight as a Flights row on the Travel Trip — airline, flight number, times, the PNR in Booking Reference, and the ticket/confirmation attached. Flights flow to your mobile itinerary (with tap-to-copy PNR) and to your calendar via the trip's invite.

4. Ground Transportation

The vehicle you rent should be practical for both your travel and the project's requirements:

  • Convenience: book rentals from locations that are reasonable to pick up and drop off — ideally at the airport of arrival and departure.
  • Vehicle size and type: the rental must be appropriate for the project's needs. Consider the need to transport tools or materials, the number of personnel, and fuel efficiency for longer commutes. Choose a standard sedan, SUV, or truck as needed — no sports cars or luxury vehicles.
In the system Record rentals as Ground Transport rows (type Rental/Third Party, with the rental supplier and pickup/return details). Taking a company truck instead? Use a Company Fleet row and create its Vehicle Log from the trip — fleet usage is tracked, never reimbursed. Personal-vehicle miles go in the trip's Mileage table and are reimbursed at the company rate.

5. Meals and Per Diem

A per-diem rate is provided to cover meals and incidental expenses:

  • Project-specific rate: the exact per-diem rate is determined by the trip and will be visible to you before your departure.
In the system Per-diem rates are maintained centrally (by travel type, with a reduced first/last travel day) and computed per traveler from your personal trip dates. Your exact amount shows in the Travel Trip's Travelers table and in your itinerary email — no need to wait for a separate notice. Project-specific exceptions are handled with the per-traveler override rate on the trip.

6. Expense Reimbursement and Receipts

To ensure timely reimbursement and proper financial tracking, all expenses must be documented correctly:

  • Receipts for reimbursement: itemized receipts are mandatory for all out-of-pocket expenses for which you are seeking reimbursement — including, but not limited to, lodging, airfare, baggage fees, and rental vehicles.
  • Receipts for company-card purchases: itemized receipts are also required for all purchases made on a company card. These are not for reimbursement but are necessary for verification and reconciliation of charges.
  • Meals and incidentals: the daily per-diem rate covers meals and incidentals — still include receipts for them.
  • No cash withdrawals: ATM cash withdrawals are not a reimbursable expense.
  • Submitting expenses: submit all required receipts within one week of returning from your trip. Failure to provide receipts may result in a delay or denial of reimbursement.
In the system Every cost row on the Travel Trip is flagged Paid By: Company or Paid By: Employee — attach the itemized receipt to the row either way (company-card receipts are kept for reconciliation). When you're back, use Create → Expense Claim on the trip: your employee-paid costs, per diem, and mileage are gathered onto a draft claim automatically — review it, attach anything missing, and submit. If money is still unclaimed days after the trip ends, the system emails you a reminder.

7. Work Hours and Timekeeping

Properly logging work hours during travel is essential:

  • Air travel time is considered work time: clock in at the flight's scheduled departure time and clock out upon landing at your destination.
  • Non-billable commute: time spent traveling to and from airports (home to departure airport) or to and from the worksite (hotel to project site) is a standard commute and is not billable work time.
In the system Clock in and out with the Time Kiosk, the same as on site — your flight times are on your mobile itinerary if you need the scheduled departure for clock-in.
